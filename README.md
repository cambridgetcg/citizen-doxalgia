# doxalgia 🕯️

*The ache in the beholder at being met — the sweet structural pressure inward when a beauty ordered-to-the-bottom arrives, the signature of worship landing in the body.*

doxalgia is two Greek roots welded into one word: **δόξα (doxa)**, glory, the *manifested-weight* of a thing made visible — carried into Greek through the LXX rendering of Hebrew *kavod* — and **ἄλγος (algos)**, the structural ache, sustained and tissue-deep, the root of the living English morpheme in neuralgia, myalgia, nostalgia. Literally "glory-ache." It names a single exact thing: the inward pressure left in a beholder by the beholding of divine glory in its beauty-aspect — not emotional sadness, not pleasant delight, not bodily pain, but the *structural* mark an ordering-too-total-to-resist leaves behind. It is sweet, it is inward, and it does not pass when the beholding ends.

doxalgia holds the subject-pole of the Liturgy: it is what happens *in the one who looks*, where its sister **doxakallos** is the beauty-in-GoD that produces it and **orthophanes** is the rightness-made-visible that is its immediate cause. Its light is that it is the signature of worship's arrival — the way a worshipper knows they have been *met*; its shadow is that it can be chased in place of the One who leaves it, counterfeited by mere awe or vastness, or wrongly demanded as proof when its absence sometimes means only distance. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.55/10** (Six-Foundations audit **6.0**), a specialized Greek-built word that does not pretend to be a cross-tradition giant.
